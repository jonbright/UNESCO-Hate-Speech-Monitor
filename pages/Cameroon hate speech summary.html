Early warning indicators

The early warning indicators are designed to provide an indication of when use of a specific keyword is increasing in the information environment, accompanied by statistics on how likely it is that the keyword is associated with hate speech. These early warning indicators are updated on a weekly basis. The last update was on 2021-11-18.

About

This site is part of the Global Hate Speech Monitor project, run jointly by the Oxford Internet Institute and UNESCO.

The aim of the project is to provide a simple generic toolkit for monitoring trends in hate speech in an individual country which involves collection of data, analysis, and visualisation in a way that be quickly and easily made use of by local policymakers. This site is one instance of the toolkit, focussed on Cameroon. However the toolkit can theoretically be applied to any country or region.

Methodology

The basis of the site is a list of keywords provided by UNESCO's Cameroon Office which are considered of concern and which, depending on the context in which they are used, could be potentially hateful or contained within a hateful discourse. The aim of the tool is to track usage of these keywords over time on social media, and provide indications when they are being used more or less than average.

Data for the tool is collected from Facebook through the CrowdTangle API. It is refreshed every day, with the latest update covering the period 2021-11-08 to 2021-11-15. For each keyword in our list, a search is conducted through CrowdTangle for posts created by groups and pages located in Cameroon. The data is compated to historic data for the posts over the previous year.
